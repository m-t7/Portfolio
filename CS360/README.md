### Briefly summarize the requirements and goals of the app you developed. What user needs was this app designed to address?
The app was designed to manage event scheduling, allowing users to log in, store event details in a database, and receive SMS reminders, addressing the need for event tracking and notifications.

### What screens and features were necessary to support user needs and produce a user-centered UI for the app? How did your UI designs keep users in mind? Why were your designs successful?
The app needed screens for user login, event management, and notification settings, with a simple UI and focused on accessibility.

### How did you approach the process of coding your app? What techniques or strategies did you use? How could those techniques or strategies be applied in the future?
I used an object-oriented approach with modular code, focusing on a clear separation of concerns, a strategy that can be applied in future apps to ensure maintainability.

### How did you test to ensure your code was functional? Why is this process important, and what did it reveal?
I used the Android Emulator to test user logins, database CRUD operations, and SMS permission handling, ensuring all features worked as expected and revealing edge cases that needed fixing.

### Consider the full app design and development process from initial planning to finalization. Where did you have to innovate to overcome a challenge?
I had to innovate in managing SMS permissions by creating fallback functionality to ensure the app functioned without interruptions if the user denied permission for notifications.

### In what specific component of your mobile app were you particularly successful in demonstrating your knowledge, skills, and experience?
I successfully implemented a SQLite database, demonstrating my ability to handle data persistence and CRUD operations in a mobile app.
